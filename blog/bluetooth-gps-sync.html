2026-05-02 GPS & Time Sync

Bluetooth GPS Sync

Syncing your system clock and grid square from an Android phone or Kenwood HT — no internet required.

Sylvain Deguire (VA2OPS) · LiaisonOS 2.2.6

Why accurate time matters in digital modes

Digital weak-signal modes like FT8, JS8Call, and VARA require your system clock to be accurate within a few seconds — ideally within one second. If your clock drifts, your transmissions fall out of the decode window and other stations simply cannot hear you.

In a field deployment or emergency activation, you may not have internet access for NTP. LiaisonOS solves this with Bluetooth GPS Sync — pulling GPS time directly from a device you likely already carry.

Supported GPS sources

📱
Android + LiaisonGPS App
Any Android phone with GPS. Install the free LiaisonGPS app, open it, and your phone becomes a Bluetooth GPS beacon.
📻
Kenwood TH-D74 / TH-D75
These HTs have a built-in GPS and stream NMEA sentences over Bluetooth natively — no app needed.

How it works

LiaisonOS connects to your GPS source over Bluetooth and reads raw NMEA sentences — specifically the $GPRMC / $GNRMC sentence which contains both the UTC time and your coordinates.

Under the hood, a small C binary (et-gps-sync) watches for the exact moment a GPS second changes — called the second boundary — by detecting when the timestamp increments between two consecutive NMEA sentences. At that precise moment it reads the host clock with microsecond resolution and calls settimeofday() to snap the system clock to GPS time.

This approach is accurate regardless of Bluetooth buffer depth or processing latency, because we are watching for the GPS second to tick rather than trusting the timestamp of a single sentence.

After sync, chrony is stopped so NTP cannot overwrite your GPS-corrected time when you later connect to a network.

Grid square update

Along with the time sync, LiaisonOS extracts your latitude and longitude from the GPS fix and automatically updates your Maidenhead grid square in WSJT-X, JS8Call, and other supported applications. No manual entry needed.

Step-by-step: Android

  1. Install the LiaisonGPS APK on your Android device.
  2. Pair your Android phone with your laptop via Bluetooth.
  3. Open LiaisonGPS on your phone and wait for a GPS fix — coordinates must be visible on screen.
  4. On LiaisonOS, open Bluetooth GPS Sync from the dashboard.
  5. Select your phone from the device list and click Connect.
  6. The sync completes in a few seconds. Your clock and grid square are updated.

Step-by-step: Kenwood TH-D74 / TH-D75

  1. Enable GPS on your radio and wait for a fix (GPS icon active, coordinates visible).
  2. Pair the radio with your laptop via Bluetooth.
  3. Open Bluetooth GPS Sync from the dashboard and select your radio.
  4. Click Connect. The sync completes automatically.
Tip: If you have synced before, the app remembers your last device and skips directly to the device selection screen.

GPS accuracy note

For best results, make sure your GPS source has had a few minutes to settle after acquiring a fix. A freshly acquired fix (10–50m accuracy) may carry a small timing error. Once the position stabilizes to a few meters, timing accuracy is excellent — well within FT8's ±1 second requirement.
