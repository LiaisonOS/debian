2026-04-01 Field Operating & POTA

et-logger — Field Logger

A fully offline POTA field logger with pre-loaded parks, park-to-park tracking, CAT control, and a built-in voice keyer — ready for the field right out of the box.

Sylvain Deguire (VA2OPS) · LiaisonOS 2.2.3

Built for POTA in the field

Parks on the Air activations happen in places where internet connectivity is rare or non-existent. Most logging software assumes you are at a desk with a reliable connection — et-logger assumes you are under a tree with a portable antenna and a battery.

Every park in the POTA database is pre-loaded into LiaisonOS. There is nothing to download, no lookup service to call, no signal required. Open et-logger, select your park, and start logging.

Key features

🌲
All POTA parks pre-loaded
The complete POTA park database is included in LiaisonOS — searchable by name, reference, or grid square, fully offline.
🔁
Park-to-park tracking
P2P QSOs are flagged automatically. When the station you work is also activating a park, et-logger records it as a park-to-park contact.
📻
CAT control
Frequency and mode are logged automatically from your radio via CAT. No manual entry — change bands and et-logger follows.
🎙️
Built-in voice keyer
Send pre-recorded CQ calls with a single click. Works with any rig — no built-in keyer required on the radio itself.

The voice keyer

For operators using older rigs without a built-in voice keyer, running CQ for hours during a POTA activation is tiring. et-logger includes a CQ voice keyer that plays a pre-recorded audio clip through your radio's audio input with one click — or on a repeating timer so you can focus on listening.

Record your CQ call once, save it, and et-logger handles the rest. PTT is keyed automatically via CAT before playback and released when the clip ends. No extra hardware, no foot switch required.

Tip: Record a few seconds of silence at the end of your CQ clip to give stations time to come back before the next transmission.

Park-to-park QSO tracking

Park-to-park contacts are one of the most rewarding parts of POTA — and one of the most tedious to track manually. When you log a QSO in et-logger and the other station is also activating a park, simply enter their park reference and the contact is automatically flagged as P2P in your log.

At the end of your activation, your ADIF export includes the P2P information in the correct fields for upload to the POTA website — no editing required.

Fully offline

et-logger does not require any internet connection at any point during an activation. Park lookups, callsign logging, frequency capture via CAT, voice keyer playback — everything runs locally. When you get home, export your log as ADIF and upload to the POTA website.
