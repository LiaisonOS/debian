et-repeater — Repeater Directory

Import your RepeaterBook search results, filter by band and mode, program your radio in one click, and let Tracker mode keep your list sorted as you move.

Sylvain Deguire (VA2OPS) · LiaisonOS 2.2.3

Your repeater list, offline

RepeaterBook is the most complete repeater database available — but it is a website. In the field, on the road, or during an emergency activation, you may not have internet access when you need it most. et-repeater solves this by letting you import your RepeaterBook search results as a CSV file and keeping them locally, ready to use anywhere.

Once imported, your repeater list is fully available offline — no account, no API key, no connectivity required.

Importing from RepeaterBook

The workflow is simple: go to repeaterbook.com, search for repeaters in your area or any region you plan to visit, and export the results as a CSV file. et-repeater imports that file directly — no conversion needed.

Multiple imports are merged automatically. You can build up your local directory over time by importing different regions, and et-repeater keeps everything organized without duplicates.

Tip: Before a trip or an event, search RepeaterBook for the region, export the CSV, and import it into et-repeater. You will have a complete local repeater directory ready before you leave home.

Filter and sort

Once your repeaters are imported, et-repeater lets you narrow the list to exactly what you need:

  • Band — 10m, 6m, 2m, 1.25m, 70cm
  • Mode — FM, DMR, D-STAR, YSF, P25, NXDN
  • Max distance — show only repeaters within a set radius of your position

The list is automatically sorted by distance from your current position — closest repeaters first. Your position is taken from your LiaisonOS user configuration (GPS coordinates or Maidenhead grid square).

Program your radio in one click

Select any repeater in the list and click Set — et-repeater programs your radio via CAT instantly: output frequency, FM mode, CTCSS tone, and repeater offset are all set in one operation. No manual entry on the radio keypad, no risk of entering the wrong tone.

This works with any CAT-capable radio configured in LiaisonOS.

Tracker mode

When you are on the move — driving to a field site, travelling through a new region, or operating from a vehicle — Tracker mode keeps your repeater list current. It periodically re-reads your GPS position and re-sorts the list by distance, so the repeaters closest to where you are right now always appear at the top.

No manual refresh needed. Enable Tracker mode and your directory stays oriented to your current location throughout the trip.

Favorites

Mark any repeater as a favorite to pin it for quick access. Favorites are preserved across sessions and survive imports — adding new CSV data never removes a repeater you have starred.

Available in LiaisonOS 2.2.3 and later · Feature developed by VA2OPS
